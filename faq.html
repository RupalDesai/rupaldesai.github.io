Frequently Asked Questions

The National Disability Insurance Scheme (NDIS) is an Australian government program designed to provide support and funding for people with disabilities. It aims to help individuals achieve their goals, enhance their quality of life, and participate more fully in their communities by offering tailored support and resources.

To be eligible for the NDIS, you must meet the following criteria:

  • Be an Australian citizen or permanent resident.
  • Be aged between 7 and 65 years.
  • Have a permanent and significant disability that affects your ability to take part in everyday activities.

For specific eligibility requirements, you can contact us or visit the NDIS website for more detailed information.

To apply for the NDIS, you need to complete an Access Request. This can be done by contacting the NDIS directly or with the help of an NDIS representative. You’ll need to provide information about your disability and how it impacts your daily life. If you need assistance with the application process, we’re here to help—just contact us.

Your NDIS plan should outline your goals, the types of support you need, and how you will use your funding. This typically includes:

  • Daily living support
  • Community participation
  • Capacity building activities
  • Any special equipment or modifications you might need

Our team can assist you in developing a comprehensive plan tailored to your needs.

The NDIS offers three main types of supports:

  • Core Supports: Assistance with daily activities, household tasks, and community participation.
  • Capacity Building Supports: Support to build skills and independence, including therapy and training.
  • Capital Supports: Funding for assistive technology and home modifications.

For more details on what’s covered, please get in touch or review your NDIS plan.

You can manage your NDIS funding by choosing your providers and deciding how to use your funds. There are several ways to manage your NDIS funds:

  • Plan Managed: A plan manager handles the administration and pays providers on your behalf.
  • Self-Managed: You manage and pay for your supports yourself.
  • Agency Managed: The NDIS pays your providers directly

Our team can help you understand these options and assist with managing your plan.

Your NDIS plan is typically reviewed annually. However, if your circumstances change or you need adjustments to your plan, you can request an early review. Regular reviews ensure that your plan continues to meet your needs and goals effectively.
